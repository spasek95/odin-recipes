Bigos (Polish Hunter's Stew)

Description

Ingredients

  • 9 cups boiling water, divided
  • 3 pounds sauerkraut; rinsed, drained, and chopped
  • 15 pitted prunes
  • 5 whole allspice berries
  • 3 bay leaves
  • 1 cup dried mushrooms
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 onions, chopped
  • 1 Polish sausage, sliced
  • 1/2 pound beef stew meat, cubed
  • 1/2 pound boneless pork shoulder, cubed
  • 1/2 cup bacon strips, diced
  • 1 tablespoon caraway seeds
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • 3/4 cup red wine
  • Optional: 3 tablespoons tomato paste

Steps

  1. Place sauerkraut in a large pan or casserole dish and pour in 4 cups of boiling water. Add prunes, allspice, and bay leaves. Simmer until sauerkraut is soft, about 50 minutes.
  2. Pour ~1 cup of boiling water over mushrooms and soak to rehydrate, about 30 minutes. Drain an chop mushrooms, preserve the liquid.
  3. Heat oil in frying pan over medium to high heat. Add onion and sausage, saute while stirring until onion is soft and sausage is brown.
  4. In a separate pan, bring about 4 cups of water to a boil. Add beef, pork, and bacon then simmer until cooked through. Drain meat.
  5. When sauerkraut is soft, add drained meat mixture, sausage-onion mixture, and soaked mushrooms; mix well. Simmer uncovered on low heat for 20 minutes.
  6. Pour in red wine and cook for 15 minutes until flavours are well blended. Season with caraway seeds, marjoram, salt, and pepper. Stir in tomato paste (if desired). If stew is too dry, pour in some of the reserved mushroom water and simmer to combine flavours.
